About ORBITA

A sovereign space communications infrastructure provider founded to close the connectivity gap. From a vision of uninterrupted global coverage to a multi-orbit constellation in operation.

Who We Are

Mission & Vision

ORBITA was established to build and operate the world's most resilient space-based communications network — owned and governed independently to guarantee access for sovereign nations, allied defence partners, and enterprise customers alike.

Our vision is a fully interconnected planet where no point on Earth is more than 30 milliseconds from any other — enabled by a unified multi-orbit fabric spanning LEO, MEO, GEO, and HEO regimes. We are headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, with mission operations centres in California, Bengaluru, and Dubai.

🎯

Mission Statement

To deliver sovereign, secure, and universally accessible space communications infrastructure that empowers nations, enterprises, and humanity's expansion beyond Earth.

🔭

Our Founding Story

Founded in 2018 by a consortium of telecommunications engineers, former NASA mission directors, and international policy experts who recognised that the world needed a neutral, non-aligned space communications utility — free from single-nation dependency.

Our Journey

Timeline of Achievement

2018

Foundation

ORBITA incorporated in Geneva. Initial Series A funding of $420M. Ground station site surveys begin across 14 countries.

2020

First Ground Station

Alpha Prime station operational in Mojave, California. ITU spectrum filings submitted for Ka, Ku, and V-band constellations.

2022

GEO Relay Launch

ORBITA-GEO-001 and 002 launched via Falcon 9 to 68.5°E and 103°W. First transponder leases activated for DTH and government services.

2024

LEO Shell Begins

First 12 LEO demonstration satellites deployed at 550 km. Sub-30 ms latency validated across transatlantic links.

2026

Global Coverage

148 satellites operational across all four orbital regimes. 32 ground stations online. Service available in 187 countries.
